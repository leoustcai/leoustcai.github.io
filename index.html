Team

The people shaping the work.

This is not a bloated org chart. It is a working group with clear ownership: direction, frontend craft, backend delivery, and structural judgment.

A small crew with distinct jobs and strong opinions.

Leo sets the bar and calls direction. The rest of the team keeps that direction honest by turning it into interfaces, workflows, and implementation that can survive real use.

The point is not to look busy. The point is to keep the body of work coherent, practical, and worth showing in public.

Projects

The showcase is the work itself.

These entries come straight from the live projects folder. The mix is deliberate: one flagship product, a few strong utilities, and a handful of supporting builds that keep the whole studio moving.

Flagship product

OpenClaw

A personal AI assistant that lives on Leo's own devices and shows up through the channels he already uses. It is the biggest bet in the folder and the clearest statement of the studio's ambition.

Why it exists Make personal AI feel local, practical, and always within reach.
Developer tool

git2

A lightweight Python wrapper around the system Git binary with one custom branch handoff workflow. It keeps the command surface familiar while cutting maintenance cost hard.

Why it exists Keep daily Git work fast without carrying a heavy fork.
Workflow research

git2-upstream-sync

A deeper fork that tracks upstream Git while exploring a shared filesystem model for collaborative agent work. It is part experiment, part infrastructure study.

Why it exists Test the edges of a multi-worker Git workflow without throwing compatibility away.
Public presence

mywebsite

The studio front page. A clean place to explain who is building, what is shipping, and why the body of work makes sense when seen together rather than one repo at a time.

Why it exists Give the work a sharper public frame than a raw directory listing ever could.
Work demo

leoclaw-github

A smaller outward-facing demo space. It functions like a proving ground for showing work clearly and keeping the public footprint aligned with the actual projects in motion.

Why it exists Practice the public presentation layer instead of leaving it as an afterthought.
Skill library

skills-collection

A working shelf of reusable skills and operating notes gathered from real project work. It captures what has proven useful enough to keep using instead of reinventing every time.

Why it exists Turn good repeated practice into something the team can reuse with less friction.
Approach

How the team keeps the work sharp.

The best projects here do not come from more layers. They come from fast decisions, clear ownership, and a refusal to ship vague work.

One

Direction first.

Start by deciding what the project should be, not by collecting parts. A strong page or tool is easier to build than rescue.

Two

Small ownership.

Each area has a clear lead. That keeps the work readable, cuts drift, and makes tradeoffs easier to call when time matters.

Three

Public proof.

The standard is not whether a project sounds smart in a note. It is whether someone can open it, use it, and understand why it is there.
